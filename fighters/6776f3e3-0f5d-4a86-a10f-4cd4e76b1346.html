Fighter Backstory

Karl Mercer grew up where the sump docks sweat rust and the lights fail just long enough to get people killed. Most kids learned to run. Karl learned to watch. He noticed when footsteps changed pace, when a voice got too calm, when a corridor went quiet for the wrong reason. In the underhive, seeing trouble first is almost the same thing as beating it.

His eyes became a story because they stayed bright in a place that grinds brightness out of everyone. Some call it mutation, some luck, some omen. Karl shrugs and keeps scanning corners.

He joined the Black Luggers after warning an Orlock patrol about a Delaque ambush seconds before the knives came out. Three gangers lived because of that warning, and Grinnarg remembered. As a Gunner and true ganger, Karl anchors the middle of the chain: no glory rank, no command patch, just relentless competence. Isaac trusts his threat calls. Tyra trusts his restraint. Hank trusts him near rattled prospects because Karl explains danger without theatrics.

The spinal injury should have ended his field work. It did not. He moves stiffer now, but his shots are cleaner, almost patient enough to look cold. Raphael works best beside him. Joy still hears greenhorn jokes from older hands, yet Karl has started feeding her quiet corrections over comms because he knows potential when he sees it.

He rarely talks about loyalty. He shows it by taking one extra watch and pretending he just could not sleep.

Mercer still calls himself a lookout before anything else, even now that his marksmanship could earn louder titles. Maybe that humility is the point. He believes the gang survives by seeing danger early, not by pretending danger can be outshouted. So he keeps watching, keeps warning, keeps choosing the unglamorous job that everyone depends on.
