Abilities

Rule of Iron:

Rule of Iron: At activation, give a visible, friendly Orlock fighter within 6" Nerves of Steel until end of round. In addition, while on the battlefield. subtract 2 from the result of any Bottle test.

Iron Stare:

Iron Stare: Enemy fighters wishing to target this fighter with a ranged attack must first pass a Cool check if this fighter has a line of sight to that enemy fighter. In addition, this fighter never counts as the closest fighter for the purposes of Target Priority.

Shotgun Savant:

Shotgun Savant: With any shotgun type, may use Short Acc. modifier at Long range; Scattershot: hit count, roll 2D6 and choose the highest.

Fighter Backstory

Hank Dent is the kind of man who can quiet a room by leaning in a doorway. No threats. No speech. Just that unreadable stare and the feeling that if you take one wrong step, he already knows how the next ten minutes end.

He earned it in the foundries, where heat peels weakness off people fast. Before the gang had a name worth fearing, Hank learned to keep his hands steady while metal screamed and men panicked. Later, during the Foundry Purge, creditors came loud and armed to seize an Orlock route. Hank met them at the gate with his shotgun down and his eyes up. They backed off without a single trigger pull. The story spread. So did the name Iron Stare.

Inside the Black Luggers, Hank is champion tier and backbone both. Grinnarg sets course; Hank keeps everyone from drifting off it. Prospects answer to him first, which means Charly gets pushed hard, Andy gets reined in, and Barnabas gets his “creative ideas” inspected before deployment. Isaac respects him because Hank never confuses bravery with good odds. Tyra relies on him when bottle checks start looming and nerves go thin.

Around the fire, he notices what others miss: who is skipping meals, who stopped joking, who keeps checking exits mid-conversation. He fixes small fractures before they become funerals. In a gang full of loud legends, Hank is the weight that keeps the floor from giving way.

He is also the gang's last checkpoint before violence becomes waste. If Hank says wait, even Isaac waits. If Hank says push, prospects stop shaking and move. There is comfort in that, though nobody says it out loud. In a place built on noise and panic, Hank gives the Black Luggers something rare: a center that does not move.

That is why Grinnarg trusts him with the ugly conversations after a bad run. Hank can tell a fighter hard truth without humiliating them, and he can do it in ten words or less. No ceremony. Just course correction. The gang walks out bruised, quieter, and better than they walked in.
