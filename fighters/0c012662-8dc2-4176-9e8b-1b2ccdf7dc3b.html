Fighter Backstory

Jonathan Hoover didn’t earn the nickname “Wiseguy” because he was clever. He earned it because he never stops talking. In the underhive, where most folks communicate with grunts, threats, or gunfire, Hoover’s constant stream of commentary stands out like a malfunctioning sump pump. He’s the kind of man who can haggle, insult, flatter, and distract—all in the same breath—and somehow walk away with a profit.

Hoover grew up around ammo stalls and back‑alley gunsmiths, learning early that bullets are worth more than loyalty and almost as much as clean water. He never had the muscle to be a proper ganger, but he had something just as valuable: connections. He knows every ammo‑peddler, every smuggler, every half‑legit bullet press from here to the ash gates. If someone in the hive is selling rounds, Hoover knows what they cost, who they owe, and how to get them cheaper.

The Dents brought him in after he saved Isaac’s life during a firefight—not by shooting straight, but by tossing Isaac a handful of manstopper rounds at the perfect moment. Isaac dropped three enemies in seconds. Hoover just winked and said, “Right ammo for the right moment, boys.”

As a Bullet Merchant, Hoover keeps the Black Lugger guns fed and their enemies guessing. His Fast Shot talent is less about accuracy and more about volume—he fires like he talks: too much, too fast, and somehow effective. His autopistol is battered, loud, and unreliable, but in Hoover’s hands it becomes a punctuation mark to whatever wisecrack he’s making.

He’s not a fighter, not really. But he’s useful. And in the underhive, useful people live longer than brave ones.
