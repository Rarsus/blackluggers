Fighter Backstory

Barnabas Keel loves explosions with the devotion of a poet and the judgment of a child left alone in an ammunition locker. “Boom Boom” started as mockery after he collapsed half a sump tunnel during his first year on hauling crews. He walked out coughing soot and grinning like he had discovered religion.

The Black Luggers took him in because talent like that is too dangerous to leave wandering. As a prospect, Barnabas sits at the bottom of formal command and at the center of everyone’s blood pressure. Hank checks his charges personally. Isaac makes him drill release timing until his arm shakes. Tyra lectures him on structural integrity like a disappointed schoolmistress. Grinnarg keeps him because, on the right day, Barnabas can solve a problem no boltgun can.

He is louder than Charly, less disciplined than Andy, and far more technically gifted than either likes to admit. Joy became an unexpected mentor, forcing him to map blast cones and casualty arcs after one near-disaster in a tight corridor. Karl distrusts him around load-bearing supports. Raphael distrusts him around anything flammable. Barnabas takes both as compliments.

The truth beneath the chaos: he studies. Old charge manuals, failed fuses, pressure tables, bad wiring diagrams-he reads all of it and remembers too much. When focus locks in, he can place one demo charge like a signature and open exactly the hole the gang needs.

If he survives his own enthusiasm, Barnabas will be terrifying. For now, he is terrifying in progress.

Keel jokes through fear because silence makes him think too hard about blast failures and bodies. But beneath the jokes, he has begun choosing precision over spectacle. One charge, one objective, one clean retreat route. The champions notice. So does Charly. Even Andy has started waiting for Barnabas's count before he sprints into the breach.
