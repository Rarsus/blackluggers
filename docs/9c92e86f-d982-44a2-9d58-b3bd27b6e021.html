Too Pretty For Primus:

Too Pretty For Primus: If this fighter is still on the battlefield at the end of a battle, their gang adds 2D6x5 credits to their Stash. However, if a double is rolled, these credits are added to your opponent's Stash instead.

Fixer:

Fixer: In the Receive Rewards step of the post-battle sequence, as long as this model is not Captured or In Recovery, their gang earns an additional D3x10 credits. Note that they do not need to have taken part in the battle to gain this bonus.

Fighter Backstory

Grinnarg Dent never pretended the hive was fair. He learned early that fists win moments, but leverage wins years. The nickname Pretty Face started as a joke from dockworkers who thought he smiled too much for an Orlock. Then they watched him talk a foreman into handing over his own blackmail ledger and stopped laughing.

That was the Ledger Heist, the night the Black Luggers became more than a hard-luck crew with good guns. Grinnarg walked in alone, left with proof of skimming, and did it without firing a shot. Hank called it dangerous. Isaac called it elegant. Tyrell just said, “It worked,” and started calculating how many routes they could hold with the new income.

As Road Captain, Grinnarg sits at the top of the chain, but he runs the gang like a machine with living parts. Hank keeps discipline. Isaac breaks stalemates. Tyra steadies the line when panic starts. Karl and Raphael hold the long grind. Joy is still treated like a greenhorn by some old hands, though Grinnarg has started giving her jobs he only gives people he trusts. The prospects-Charly, Andy, Barnabas-are fire in three different forms, and Grinnarg’s talent is turning that fire outward instead of letting it burn the gang from inside.

He is charming when he needs to be, ruthless when he must be, and honest only when honesty is the sharpest blade available. In the underhive, that is close enough to leadership to look like destiny.

The crew jokes that Grinnarg can smell betrayal through sheet metal. It is not magic, just attention: who avoids eye contact when credits are counted, who talks too fast after a meeting, who suddenly offers a favor for free. He files every detail away. When pressure hits, those details become leverage, and leverage keeps his people breathing.

Even now, he still brings the clean ledger slate to "Granny" Dent first after a profitable run. If she gives that tiny nod over her tea tin, Grinnarg treats it like better praise than anything the hive can offer.
