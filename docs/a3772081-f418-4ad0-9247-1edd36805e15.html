Fighter Backstory

Joy Crescent didn’t grow up with her name. In fact, no one remembers what she was called before the explosion that carved a permanent half‑moon scar across her cheek. The medicae who patched her up joked that the wound “looked like a crescent,” and the name stuck. The Joy part came later—an ironic twist, because she almost never smiles. But when she does, it’s usually right after something explodes.

Joy was born in the lower manufactoria levels, where the air tastes like burnt propellant and the walls shake with conveyor engines. She learned early that survival meant understanding machinery, especially the kind that goes boom. While other kids played with scrap toys, Joy dismantled blasting charges to see how they ticked. By the time she was a teenager, she could assemble a grenade launcher blindfolded and had a reputation for turning bad situations into smoking craters.

The Dents recruited her after she single‑handedly stopped a Delaque ambush by firing a frag round into a collapsing walkway, dropping half the attackers into a sump pit. Grinnarg called it reckless. Hank called it lucky. Isaac called it beautiful. Joy just shrugged and reloaded.

In the Black Lugger gang, Joy is the quiet center of chaos. She moves with deliberate calm, checks her launcher twice before every shot, and speaks only when she has something worth saying. Her aim is steady, her timing uncanny, and her sense of distance almost supernatural. When the sump monster attacked, Joy’s grenades were the first to actually make it bleed—earning her a rare nod of respect from Hank.

She’s not loud, she’s not flashy, but when the Luggers need a problem solved at range—or erased entirely—Joy Crescent is the one they trust to pull the pin.
